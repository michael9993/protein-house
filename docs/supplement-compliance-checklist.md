## 1. Israel-Specific Regulations (Primary Market)

### 1.1 Ministry of Health (MOH) Requirements

Israel regulates dietary supplements primarily through the **Ministry of Health's Food and Nutrition Services** and the **Pharmacy Division**. Key framework:

- **Registration requirement:** Dietary supplements sold in Israel must be registered with the MOH or hold an import permit. The MOH maintains a registry of approved supplements.
- **Classification matters:** Israel distinguishes between:
  - **Food supplements** (regulated under food laws) - vitamins, minerals, amino acids, herbs in standard doses
  - **Parapharmaceuticals** - products with stronger health claims, regulated more like OTC drugs
  - If a product makes therapeutic claims, it may be reclassified as a **pharmaceutical** and require drug registration
- **Import license:** Required for importing supplements. Apply through the MOH Import Division. Processing time: 30-90 days.
- **Israeli Standard SI 993:** Governs dietary supplement formulation, maximum permitted doses, and ingredient lists.
- **Approved ingredients list:** The MOH maintains a positive list of permitted ingredients and maximum daily doses. Ingredients not on the list require special approval.

### 1.2 Labeling Requirements in Hebrew

All products sold in Israel must comply with **Israeli Consumer Protection Law** and **Food Labeling Regulations**:

- [ ] **Hebrew language mandatory:** All label information must appear in Hebrew. Additional languages (English, Arabic, Russian) are permitted but Hebrew is required.
- [ ] **Required label elements:**
  - Product name including the words "תוסף תזונה" (dietary supplement)
  - Complete list of ingredients in Hebrew (רשימת רכיבים)
  - Net quantity/weight (כמות נטו)
  - Serving size and number of servings (מנה מומלצת)
  - Nutritional information per serving
  - Manufacturer/importer name and address in Israel
  - Country of origin (ארץ מקור)
  - Expiration date (תאריך תפוגה) in DD/MM/YYYY format
  - Storage instructions (הוראות אחסון)
  - Batch/lot number (מספר אצווה)
- [ ] **Allergen declarations** in Hebrew for all major allergens (milk, soy, gluten, nuts, etc.)

### 1.3 Required Hebrew Disclaimers

The following disclaimers must appear on product labels and/or the website (in Hebrew):

```
"תוסף תזונה אינו תחליף לתזונה מגוונת ומאוזנת ולאורח חיים בריא."
(A dietary supplement is not a substitute for a varied, balanced diet and healthy lifestyle.)

"אין לחרוג מהמנה היומית המומלצת."
(Do not exceed the recommended daily dose.)

"יש לשמור מחוץ להישג ידם של ילדים."
(Keep out of reach of children.)

"מומלץ להתייעץ עם רופא/ת לפני השימוש, במיוחד בעת הריון, הנקה, או נטילת תרופות."
(It is recommended to consult a doctor before use, especially during pregnancy, breastfeeding, or when taking medications.)
```

For products containing caffeine:
```
"מכיל קפאין. לא מומלץ לילדים, נשים בהריון או מניקות ולאנשים הרגישים לקפאין."
(Contains caffeine. Not recommended for children, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and people sensitive to caffeine.)
```

### 1.4 Import Regulations for Supplements

- [ ] **Import permit** from MOH required for each product/SKU
- [ ] **Free Sale Certificate (FSC)** from the country of manufacture may be required
- [ ] **Certificate of Analysis (COA)** for each batch
- [ ] **GMP certificate** of the manufacturing facility
- [ ] **Customs broker** - recommended for navigating Israeli customs (Mekhinsa)
- [ ] Products must clear **Israeli Standards Institute (ISI/SII)** testing if requested
- [ ] **Personal import exemption:** Individuals may import up to 3 months' supply for personal use without MOH approval (relevant for understanding customer expectations)

### 1.5 Banned/Restricted Ingredients in Israel

Israel's MOH maintains a stricter ingredient policy than the US. The following are **banned or restricted**:

| Ingredient | Status in Israel | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Ephedra / Ephedrine** | Banned | Classified as pharmaceutical |
| **DMAA (1,3-dimethylamylamine)** | Banned | Not permitted in supplements |
| **DMHA** | Banned | Not approved |
| **Yohimbine** (high dose) | Restricted | Requires pharmaceutical classification above certain doses |
| **Synephrine** (high dose) | Restricted | Limited to 30mg/day |
| **Androstenedione** | Banned | Prohormone, classified as drug |
| **SARMs** | Banned | Not approved for sale |
| **CBD/THC** | Banned in supplements | Cannabis-derived ingredients require separate licensing |
| **DHEA** | Restricted | May require prescription |
| **Kava Kava** | Restricted | MOH warnings issued |
| **Melatonin** | Restricted | In some formulations requires prescription |
| **High-dose caffeine** (pills >200mg/unit) | Restricted | Special warnings required |

### 1.6 Customs & Duty Considerations

- **Customs duty:** Dietary supplements typically fall under customs code 2106.90 (food preparations NES). Duty rate: 0-12% depending on specific classification.
- **VAT:** 17% VAT applies to all imports and domestic sales in Israel.
- **Purchase tax:** Generally not applicable to standard supplements but check for products containing sugar.
- **Customs clearance:** Allow 7-14 business days. Products may be held for MOH inspection.
- **Free trade agreements:** Check if manufacturer's country has an FTA with Israel (US, EU, UK, etc.) for reduced duties.

## 2. US/International Regulations (Secondary Market)

### 2.1 FDA DSHEA Requirements

The **Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act of 1994 (DSHEA)** is the primary US regulatory framework:

- [ ] **No pre-market approval required** - but manufacturer is responsible for safety and labeling accuracy
- [ ] **New Dietary Ingredient (NDI) notification** required for ingredients not marketed in the US before Oct 15, 1994
- [ ] **Adverse event reporting** - serious adverse events must be reported to FDA within 15 business days
- [ ] **FDA facility registration** - all facilities manufacturing, processing, packing, or holding supplements for US consumers must register with FDA
- [ ] **cGMP compliance** - manufacturing must follow Current Good Manufacturing Practices under **21 CFR Part 111**
- [ ] **Product liability insurance** - strongly recommended; most retailers require $2M-$5M coverage

### 2.2 Required Supplement Facts Panel Format

Every US-market supplement must include a **Supplement Facts panel** (21 CFR 101.36):

```
+-------------------------------------------+
|          Supplement Facts                  |
|-------------------------------------------+
| Serving Size: 1 Scoop (30g)               |
| Servings Per Container: 30                |
|-------------------------------------------+
|                        Amount    % Daily   |
|                       Per Serving  Value   |
|-------------------------------------------+
| Calories               120                |
| Total Carbohydrate      3g        1%      |
| Protein                24g       48%      |
| Vitamin D              20mcg    100%      |
|-------------------------------------------+
| * Percent Daily Values are based on a     |
|   2,000 calorie diet.                     |
| ** Daily Value not established.           |
+-------------------------------------------+
```

**Required elements:**
- [ ] "Supplement Facts" heading (not "Nutrition Facts")
- [ ] Serving size and servings per container
- [ ] Amount per serving for each dietary ingredient
- [ ] % Daily Value where established (based on updated Reference Daily Intakes)
- [ ] List of all dietary ingredients
- [ ] "Other ingredients" listed below the panel in descending order of predominance
- [ ] Name and address of manufacturer, packer, or distributor
- [ ] Minimum font size requirements (6-point for most info)

### 2.3 Required FDA Disclaimer

When making structure/function claims, the following disclaimer is **legally required** and must appear:

```
"These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration.
This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
```

**Placement rules:**
- [ ] Must appear on the product label where the claim is made
- [ ] Must be in **boldface type** on the label
- [ ] On websites: must appear on the same page as any structure/function claim
- [ ] Must be in close proximity to the claim, not buried in footer
- [ ] Notification to FDA: must submit structure/function claim text within **30 days** of first marketing

### 2.4 FTC Advertising Rules for Supplement Claims

The **Federal Trade Commission (FTC)** governs supplement advertising (separate from FDA labeling):

- [ ] **Substantiation requirement:** All claims must be truthful, non-deceptive, and substantiated by "competent and reliable scientific evidence"
- [ ] **"Adequate and well-controlled" studies** generally required for health-related claims
- [ ] **Testimonials/endorsements** must reflect typical results; if atypical, must disclose typical results
- [ ] **"Clinically proven" claims** require at least one well-designed clinical trial
- [ ] **Cannot claim to cure, treat, or prevent disease** (that makes it a drug claim)
- [ ] **Money-back guarantees** do not absolve responsibility for false claims
- [ ] **Fines:** FTC can impose fines up to $50,120 per violation (as of 2025, adjusted for inflation)

**Permitted vs. prohibited claim examples:**

| Permitted (Structure/Function) | Prohibited (Disease Claim) |
|---|---|
| "Supports immune health" | "Prevents colds and flu" |
| "Promotes joint comfort" | "Treats arthritis" |
| "Supports healthy testosterone levels" | "Cures low testosterone" |
| "Helps maintain healthy blood sugar" | "Lowers blood sugar in diabetics" |
| "Supports muscle recovery" | "Heals muscle tears" |
| "Promotes restful sleep" | "Treats insomnia" |

### 2.5 California Proposition 65

Prop 65 (Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986) requires warnings for products containing chemicals known to cause cancer or reproductive toxicity:

- [ ] **Applies if selling to California residents** (i.e., any US e-commerce store)
- [ ] **Warning required** if product contains listed chemicals above "safe harbor" levels
- [ ] **Common supplement triggers:** Lead, cadmium, arsenic, mercury (trace contaminants in herbs/minerals), acrylamide, BPA (from packaging)

**Required warning format (post-August 2018):**
```
⚠ WARNING: This product can expose you to chemicals including [chemical name],
which is known to the State of California to cause [cancer/birth defects or other
reproductive harm]. For more information go to www.P65Warnings.ca.gov.
```

- [ ] Must include the **yellow triangle warning symbol** on physical labels
- [ ] On websites: must be displayed **prior to purchase** (product page, not just checkout)
- [ ] **Safe harbor levels** for common contaminants (check current OEHHA list):
  - Lead: 0.5 mcg/day
  - Cadmium: 4.1 mcg/day
  - Arsenic (inorganic): no safe harbor level established
- [ ] **Testing recommended:** Third-party testing for heavy metals to determine if warning is needed
- [ ] **Penalties:** Up to $2,500 per violation per day; private "bounty hunter" lawsuits are common

**Recent developments (2025):**
- Acrylamide in food/beverage products: Currently subject to court injunction limiting enforcement
- Titanium dioxide in cosmetics/personal care: Enforcement enjoined as of August 2025
- Always check current status of enforcement actions

### 2.6 Age Restrictions

No federal US age restriction exists for most supplements, but **best practices and state laws**:

- [ ] **Pre-workout (high caffeine):** Display "Not intended for persons under 18" prominently
- [ ] **Testosterone boosters:** Add age restriction (18+) at checkout and on product pages
- [ ] **Weight-loss supplements:** 18+ recommended
- [ ] **Creatine:** While not legally restricted, recommend 18+ labeling per industry standards
- [ ] **Israel:** No specific age restrictions for supplement purchase, but products containing caffeine should carry warnings about children

## 3. E-Commerce Legal Requirements

### 3.1 Terms of Service - Supplement-Specific Provisions

Your ToS must include provisions beyond standard e-commerce terms:

- [ ] **Health disclaimer:** Products are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease
- [ ] **Assumption of risk:** Customer assumes responsibility for verifying product suitability
- [ ] **Consult healthcare provider:** Encourage customers to consult a physician before use
- [ ] **Allergen disclaimer:** Despite best efforts, cross-contamination may occur
- [ ] **Accuracy of information:** Product information is provided by manufacturers; you relay it in good faith
- [ ] **Prohibited use clause:** Products sold for personal use only, not for resale
- [ ] **Age restriction clause:** Confirm buyer is 18+ (especially for pre-workouts, testosterone boosters)
- [ ] **Indemnification clause:** Customer indemnifies seller for misuse
- [ ] **Limitation of liability:** Cap liability at product purchase price
- [ ] **Jurisdiction:** Specify governing law (Israeli law for Israeli market, relevant US state law for US)

### 3.2 Return/Refund Policies

Israel and US have different requirements:

**Israel (Consumer Protection Law 5741-1981):**
- [ ] **14-day cooling-off period** for online purchases (distance transactions) - customer can cancel for any reason
- [ ] **Opened supplements:** You **may** refuse returns of opened supplement containers for health/hygiene reasons, but must clearly state this policy before purchase
- [ ] **Unopened supplements:** Must accept returns within 14 days
- [ ] **Refund timeline:** Within 14 days of receiving the returned product
- [ ] **Cancellation fee:** May charge up to 5% of transaction or 100 ILS (whichever is lower) for cancellation

**United States:**
- [ ] **No federal law** mandates a specific return policy for supplements
- [ ] **State laws vary:** Some states require return policies to be clearly posted
- [ ] **Industry standard:** 30-day money-back guarantee (common for supplements)
- [ ] **Opened products:** Most supplement companies accept returns of opened products (competitive standard) but you are not legally required to
- [ ] **Chargebacks:** A too-strict policy increases chargeback risk (see Section 4)

**Recommended dual-market policy:**
```
Israel: 14-day return for unopened products per Israeli law. Opened products
non-returnable for hygiene/safety (clearly disclosed before purchase).

US: 30-day satisfaction guarantee. Opened products accepted for return
(up to one used container). Customer pays return shipping.
```

### 3.3 Subscription Auto-Renewal Laws

If offering subscribe-and-save, compliance is critical:

**United States:**
- [ ] **FTC Negative Option Rule (updated 2024):** Requires:
  - Clear disclosure of all material terms BEFORE obtaining billing info
  - Explicit informed consent (separate from other terms)
  - **Simple cancellation mechanism** - must be as easy to cancel as to sign up ("click-to-cancel")
  - Cannot require calling a phone number if signup was online
- [ ] **California ARL (Auto-Renewal Law, Bus. & Prof. Code 17600-17606):**
  - Clear and conspicuous disclosure before purchase
  - Affirmative consent
  - Acknowledgment/confirmation email with cancellation policy
  - **Online cancellation** must be offered if signup was online
  - Free trial conversions require additional reminder notice
- [ ] **New York, Illinois, Vermont, Colorado** have similar state-specific laws
- [ ] **Penalties:** California ARL violations: up to $2,500/violation + restitution

**Israel:**
- [ ] **Consumer Protection Regulations (Cancellation of Transaction):** Customer may cancel recurring transactions at any time
- [ ] Must provide cancellation mechanism via **the same channel used for signup** (if online signup, online cancellation required)
- [ ] Renewal notice recommended before each charge

**Implementation checklist:**
- [ ] Pre-purchase: Clear disclosure of price, frequency, and total commitment
- [ ] Signup: Separate checkbox for auto-renewal consent (not pre-checked)
- [ ] Confirmation email with renewal terms and cancellation instructions
- [ ] Reminder email 3-7 days before each renewal charge
- [ ] Self-service cancellation in account settings
- [ ] No dark patterns (hidden cancel buttons, guilt-trip flows, etc.)

### 3.4 Age Verification

- [ ] **Soft verification** (date-of-birth entry or checkbox) is sufficient for supplements in most jurisdictions
- [ ] **No hard verification** (ID upload) required for supplements (unlike alcohol/tobacco)
- [ ] Add age gate (18+ checkbox) at checkout for: pre-workouts, testosterone boosters, fat burners
- [ ] Store age confirmation in order record for compliance documentation

### 3.5 GDPR / Privacy for Health-Related Purchases

Supplement purchase data can be considered **health-related data** under privacy laws:

**GDPR (EU/EEA customers):**
- [ ] **Special category data:** Purchase of supplements related to specific health conditions (testosterone, joint pain, etc.) may constitute health data under Article 9
- [ ] **Legal basis:** Explicit consent or contractual necessity
- [ ] **Privacy policy** must disclose: what data is collected, why, who it's shared with, retention period
- [ ] **Right to erasure:** Customers can request deletion of their data
- [ ] **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** required with all third-party processors (Shopify, analytics, email providers)
- [ ] **Cookie consent** banner required for EU visitors

**Israel Privacy Protection Law (5741-1981) & Regulations:**
- [ ] Register database with the **Israeli Privacy Protection Authority (PPA)** if storing data of 10,000+ individuals
- [ ] Customer consent required for marketing communications
- [ ] Right to access and correct personal data
- [ ] Data breach notification obligations

**US Privacy Laws:**
- [ ] **No federal health-data privacy law** for e-commerce (HIPAA applies only to healthcare providers)
- [ ] **California CCPA/CPRA:** Right to know, delete, opt-out of sale. Health data is "sensitive personal information" requiring opt-in consent for non-essential use
- [ ] **State health data laws** (Washington My Health My Data Act, Connecticut, etc.): May apply to supplement purchase data
- [ ] **COPPA:** If any chance of under-13 users, do not collect personal data without parental consent

## 4. Payment Processing Considerations

### 4.1 High-Risk Classification

**Yes, supplements are generally classified as "high-risk" by payment processors.** Reasons:

- Higher chargeback rates than average e-commerce (industry average: 1-3% vs general e-commerce 0.5-1%)
- Regulatory scrutiny and potential for product claims issues
- Subscription/recurring billing model increases dispute risk
- History of fraudulent operators in the space

### 4.2 Stripe

Stripe **does** allow supplement sales but with conditions:

- [ ] Supplements are NOT on Stripe's prohibited list
- [ ] Supplements are on Stripe's **restricted** list requiring additional review
- [ ] **Requirements for approval:**
  - Business must be legitimate and registered
  - Products must comply with applicable regulations (FDA, etc.)
  - No sale of controlled substances or unapproved pharmaceuticals
  - Accurate product descriptions
  - Clear refund/return policy
- [ ] **Stripe Radar** recommended for fraud prevention
- [ ] **3D Secure** recommended to reduce chargebacks
- [ ] Expected processing fees: 2.9% + $0.30 (US), may be higher for international
- [ ] **Reserve requirement:** Stripe may hold a rolling reserve (5-10% of monthly volume) for new supplement businesses

### 4.3 PayPal

PayPal has specific rules for supplements:

- [ ] Supplements **require pre-approval** from PayPal before accepting payments
- [ ] Listed under "Activities Requiring Approval" in PayPal's Acceptable Use Policy
- [ ] **Apply through:** PayPal Merchant Services, provide product details and compliance documentation
- [ ] Products must not contain controlled substances
- [ ] Claims must comply with FDA/FTC requirements
- [ ] Higher scrutiny on subscription-based supplement sales

### 4.4 Chargeback Risks & Mitigation

| Risk Factor | Mitigation Strategy |
|---|---|
| Product didn't work | Set realistic expectations in marketing; offer satisfaction guarantee proactively |
| Subscription they forgot about | Send reminder emails 3-7 days before charge; easy cancel flow |
| Didn't recognize charge | Use clear billing descriptor (e.g., "PROTEINHOUSE" not "PH LLC") |
| Product not received | Provide tracking; insure shipments; offer immediate reshipping |
| Fraudulent orders | Use 3D Secure, AVS, CVV verification; Stripe Radar or Signifyd |
| Product arrived damaged | Clear photos required for claims; ship with adequate packaging |

**Target chargeback rate: Below 0.75%** (Visa/Mastercard threshold is 1%; exceeding triggers penalties)

**Additional recommendations:**
- [ ] Use **chargeback alert services** (Ethoca, Verifi) to resolve disputes before they become chargebacks
- [ ] Implement **order confirmation emails** with product images and expected delivery
- [ ] **Customer service response time:** Under 24 hours to reduce disputes
- [ ] Keep detailed records of customer consent, especially for subscriptions
- [ ] Consider a **high-risk payment processor** as backup (PayKickstart, Durango, Bankcard International Group)

## 5. Shipping Regulations

### 5.1 Powder/Liquid Shipping Restrictions

- [ ] **Powders >350g (12oz):** USPS requires additional inspection for international shipments from the US
- [ ] **Liquids:** Must be sealed, leak-proof; IATA dangerous goods rules if flammable (most supplements are fine)
- [ ] **Capsules/tablets:** Generally no special restrictions
- [ ] **Packaging requirements:**
  - Inner seal on all powder/liquid containers
  - Tamper-evident packaging required
  - Spill-proof secondary packaging for liquids
- [ ] **Carrier policies:** Check specific carrier (FedEx, UPS, DHL, Israel Post) for supplement-specific rules

### 5.2 International Shipping of Supplements

**US to Israel:**
- [ ] All products must have MOH import permit or fall under personal import exemption
- [ ] Customs declaration must accurately list contents as "dietary supplements"
- [ ] Prohibited ingredients in Israel will be seized at customs
- [ ] Hebrew labeling can be applied via sticker upon arrival in Israel (if using import agent)
- [ ] Allow for customs clearance delays (7-14 business days)

**Israel to US:**
- [ ] FDA registration of foreign facility required
- [ ] Prior Notice of imported food (supplements are food under DSHEA) required via FDA PNSI system
- [ ] Products must meet US labeling requirements
- [ ] Customs may inspect and hold shipments
- [ ] Bioterrorism Act compliance required

**General international:**
- [ ] **Australia (TGA):** Very strict; most supplements classified as "therapeutic goods" and require registration
- [ ] **EU:** Must comply with EU Food Supplement Directive (2002/46/EC); many US ingredients are not permitted
- [ ] **Canada (NNHPD):** Requires Natural Product Number (NPN) for all supplements
- [ ] **Japan:** Strict limits on many ingredients; labeling in Japanese required
- [ ] **Brazil (ANVISA):** Registration required; lengthy approval process
- [ ] **UAE/GCC:** Halal certification may be required; MOH registration in each country

### 5.3 Temperature-Sensitive Products

- [ ] **Probiotics:** Require cold-chain shipping; use insulated packaging + ice packs
- [ ] **Protein powders:** Stable at room temperature; avoid extreme heat (above 35C/95F)
- [ ] **Fish oil/omega-3:** Sensitive to heat; ship with cold packs in summer months
- [ ] **General rule:** If product label says "store in cool, dry place," ship accordingly
- [ ] **Summer shipping:** Consider 2-day shipping or cold-pack inserts for sensitive products
- [ ] **Warehouse storage:** Climate-controlled storage required; maintain records for GMP compliance

### 5.4 Countries That Restrict Supplement Imports

| Country/Region | Restriction Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Australia** | Very High | TGA registration required; many ingredients banned |
| **Canada** | High | NPN required for all products |
| **EU/EEA** | High | Must comply with EU Directive; positive ingredient list |
| **Japan** | High | Strict ingredient limits; Japanese labeling |
| **Brazil** | High | ANVISA registration; can take 6+ months |
| **China** | Very High | CFDA registration required; expensive and slow |
| **Saudi Arabia/UAE** | Medium-High | SFDA/MOH registration; halal certification |
| **Norway** | High | Not in EU but follows similar rules |
| **South Korea** | Medium-High | MFDS notification required |
| **India** | Medium | FSSAI registration; some ingredients restricted |
| **Mexico** | Medium | COFEPRIS may require health registration |

## 6. Content & Marketing Compliance

### 6.1 Health Claims - What You Can and Cannot Say

**Three categories of claims (FDA framework):**

**1. Health Claims (require FDA authorization):**
- Describe a relationship between a substance and a disease/condition
- Example: "Calcium may reduce the risk of osteoporosis"
- Require FDA pre-authorization or qualified health claim letter
- Very few approved for supplements

**2. Structure/Function Claims (permitted with disclaimer):**
- Describe how an ingredient affects body structure or function
- Example: "Supports muscle growth," "Promotes joint health"
- Must notify FDA within 30 days
- Must include the required disclaimer
- Must have substantiation on file

**3. Nutrient Content Claims:**
- Characterize the level of a nutrient (e.g., "high in protein," "good source of vitamin D")
- Must meet FDA-defined criteria for the claim

**NEVER say:**
- [ ] "Cures," "treats," "prevents," or "diagnoses" any disease
- [ ] "Clinically proven" without actual clinical trial data
- [ ] "FDA approved" (supplements are never FDA approved)
- [ ] "Doctor recommended" without documented physician endorsements
- [ ] "100% effective" or guaranteed results
- [ ] "No side effects" (cannot guarantee)
- [ ] Specific weight loss amounts ("Lose 10 pounds in 2 weeks")

**Israel-specific marketing restrictions:**
- [ ] The MOH prohibits medical/therapeutic claims for dietary supplements
- [ ] Cannot reference specific diseases in supplement marketing
- [ ] Celebrity endorsements must comply with Israeli advertising standards
- [ ] Comparative claims ("better than brand X") require substantiation under Israeli law

### 6.2 Before/After Photo Rules

- [ ] **FTC requirement:** Results shown must be typical OR you must clearly disclose typical results
  - Example: "Results not typical. Average users lost 2-3 lbs over 8 weeks."
- [ ] **Cannot manipulate photos** (lighting, angles, Photoshop) to exaggerate results
- [ ] **Must be actual users** of the product (stock photos with false claims = deceptive advertising)
- [ ] **Timeframe disclosure:** State how long transformation took
- [ ] **Disclaimer required:** "Results may vary. This product is intended to be used in conjunction with a healthy diet and regular exercise."
- [ ] **Israel:** Before/after photos in advertising must comply with the **Israeli Consumer Protection Law** prohibition on misleading advertising
- [ ] **Social media:** Same rules apply to Instagram, TikTok, Facebook posts by the brand

### 6.3 Influencer Disclosure Requirements

**US (FTC Endorsement Guides, updated 2023):**
- [ ] **Clear and conspicuous disclosure** of material connection between influencer and brand
- [ ] **#ad or #sponsored** must be visible without clicking "more" (not buried in hashtags)
- [ ] Disclosure must be in the **same language** as the endorsement
- [ ] **Video:** Disclosure must be in the video itself (superimposed text or verbal), not just in description
- [ ] **Stories/reels:** Disclosure on EACH story frame, not just the first
- [ ] **Product seeding (gifted products):** Still requires disclosure
- [ ] Influencers cannot make claims the brand itself cannot legally make

**Israel (Israeli Consumer Protection Law + Second Authority for Television and Radio):**
- [ ] Sponsored content must be labeled "פרסומת" (advertisement) or "שיתוף פעולה" (collaboration)
- [ ] Applies to social media, blogs, YouTube, podcasts
- [ ] The **Israel Consumer Council** has issued guidelines aligning with international standards
- [ ] Brand is jointly liable for non-compliant influencer content

**Platform-specific:**
- [ ] Instagram/Facebook: Use built-in "Paid Partnership" tag
- [ ] YouTube: Check "contains paid promotion" box + verbal/visual disclosure
- [ ] TikTok: Use #ad clearly visible in the video/caption

### 6.4 Email Marketing for Supplements

**CAN-SPAM Act (US):**
- [ ] Include physical postal address in every email
- [ ] Clear "unsubscribe" mechanism in every email (must be honored within 10 business days)
- [ ] Accurate "From" and "Subject" lines (no deception)
- [ ] Identify message as advertising
- [ ] No purchased email lists without proper consent documentation

**Israel (Spam Law - Amendment 40 to Communications Law):**
- [ ] **Opt-in required:** Cannot send marketing emails without prior explicit consent
- [ ] **Exception:** Existing customers who provided email during purchase can receive marketing for similar products, with opt-out option
- [ ] Every message must include sender identity and unsubscribe option
- [ ] Fines up to 1,000 ILS per unsolicited message (no cap on aggregate)

**Health data considerations:**
- [ ] **Do not segment or target** based on health conditions inferred from purchase history without explicit consent
- [ ] Avoid email subject lines that reference health conditions (e.g., "Struggling with joint pain?")
- [ ] Personalization based on purchase history (e.g., "restock your protein") is generally acceptable
- [ ] Comply with GDPR if emailing EU residents (explicit consent, data rights)

## 7. Certifications to Display

### 7.1 GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice)

**What it is:** Compliance with FDA's Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) under 21 CFR Part 111.

**What it covers:**
- Personnel qualifications and training
- Physical plant design and facilities
- Equipment and utensil standards
- Production and process controls
- Holding and distribution procedures
- Quality control procedures
- Records and recordkeeping
- Returned product handling

**Why it matters:**
- **Legally required** for US market (FDA can shut down non-compliant facilities)
- Ensures product identity, purity, strength, and composition
- Reduces contamination and labeling errors
- Common audit certifications: **NSF GMP**, **NPA GMP**, **UL GMP**

**Display recommendation:** Show "Manufactured in a GMP Certified Facility" badge on product pages.

### 7.2 NSF Certified for Sport

**What it is:** Third-party testing program by NSF International specifically designed for athletes and active individuals.

**What it covers:**
- Tests for **270+ banned substances** from major athletic organizations (WADA, NFL, MLB, etc.)
- Confirms label accuracy (what's on the label is in the product)
- Verifies no harmful contaminants
- Ongoing monitoring with unannounced audits
- Every batch tested before release

**Why it matters:**
- Trusted by **NFL, MLB, NHL, PGA Tour, LPGA, CCES, CFLPA**
- Essential if marketing to competitive athletes
- One of the most recognized certifications in sports nutrition
- As many as **1 in 10 supplements** can be contaminated with banned substances

**Cost:** $3,000-$7,000+ per product annually (varies by product complexity)

**Display recommendation:** Show NSF Certified for Sport logo on product detail pages and product labels. Link to NSF verification page.

### 7.3 Informed Sport / Informed Choice

**What it is:** Global testing and certification program run by LGC (formerly the UK Government Chemist).

**Informed Sport:**
- Every single batch tested before market release
- Tests against WADA Prohibited List
- Used by elite athletes, Olympic teams, and professional sports organizations
- Certified over 3,000+ products in 127+ countries
- 60+ years of anti-doping expertise
- ISO 17025 accredited methods

**Informed Choice:**
- Periodic batch testing (not every batch)
- Suitable for general fitness consumers
- More affordable than Informed Sport
- Still robust testing program

**Why it matters:**
- More globally recognized than NSF (especially outside North America)
- Required/recommended by many international sports organizations
- UK, European, Australian, and Asian sports federations often prefer Informed Sport

**Display recommendation:** Show Informed Sport or Informed Choice logo. Include batch verification link.

### 7.4 USP Verified

**What it is:** The United States Pharmacopeia (USP) Verified Mark indicates a product has been tested by USP.

**What it covers:**
- Product contains ingredients listed on label in declared potency and amounts
- Product does not contain harmful levels of contaminants
- Product will break down and release into the body within specified time
- Product was manufactured according to FDA cGMP and USP standards

**Why it matters:**
- USP is the **most authoritative** standard-setting organization for medicines and supplements in the US
- Federal law requires prescription drugs to meet USP standards; voluntary for supplements
- **Highly trusted** by healthcare professionals and pharmacists
- The gold standard for supplement quality verification

**Cost:** Significant investment ($50,000+ for initial verification; varies by product)

**Display recommendation:** USP Verified Mark on product pages with explanation of what it means. Note: you must have actual USP verification - do NOT display the mark without it.

| **Kosher (OU, OK, Star-K)** | Meets kosher dietary standards | Israeli market, observant consumers |

| **Vegan Certified** | No animal-derived ingredients | Plant-based products |

**For the Israeli market specifically:**
